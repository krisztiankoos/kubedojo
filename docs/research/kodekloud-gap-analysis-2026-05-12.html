KodeKloud Engineer × kubedojo — gap analysis

Captured 2026-05-12. Sources: live curriculum at engineer.kodekloud.com/curriculum + kubedojo content tree (src/content/docs/, 648 EN files). Analyst: claude (KK pull, kubedojo grep, classification per cluster). Tracks: MLOps (this version), Cloud-AWS · Cloud-Azure · DevOps · CNCF Landscape.

TL;DR. KodeKloud's 100-day arcs surface production micro-skills (uv lockfile debugging, Vault KV policies for MLflow, drift-triggered retraining loops, multi-stage Docker for ML serving) at task-grain. kubedojo teaches the matching concepts at module-grain, but several concrete tools/patterns are absent or under-deep. Top-3 net gaps from MLOps: DVC (zero dedicated coverage, KK spends 10 days on it), Great Expectations (3 file mentions, no module), ML repo hygiene (uv + Makefile + pre-commit + cookiecutter + packaging as a connected story, currently scattered across prerequisites).
Sections: Methodology MLOps Cloud-AWS (pending) Cloud-Azure (pending) DevOps (pending) CNCF Landscape Expansion plan

Methodology

For each KodeKloud day-list, classify by topic cluster (the 100 days collapse to ~14 clusters per track). For each cluster, run a coverage check against kubedojo content:

✅ Covered — kubedojo has a dedicated module that teaches the topic at depth. ⚠️ Partial — concept mentioned or covered as part of a broader module; depth audit may surface holes. ❌ Gap — no kubedojo coverage, or coverage is shallow enough that a learner can't operate the tool from it.
Important framing. KodeKloud is a task-practice curriculum; their day-numbering serves their sandbox economy, not pedagogy. Treat their list as a checklist of tools and patterns, not a teaching roadmap. We adapt the framing: kubedojo teaches concepts before tasks (per feedback_teaching_not_listicles.md); KodeKloud's task-list shape must not leak into our module shape. Use this analysis to identify candidate gaps, then validate via Stage-2 ab discuss before opening epics.
