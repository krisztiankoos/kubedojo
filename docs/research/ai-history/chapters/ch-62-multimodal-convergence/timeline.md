# Timeline: Chapter 62 - Multimodal Convergence

- **2021-03:** CLIP appears publicly as a language-supervised visual model.
  The paper reports training on 400M image-text pairs and frames natural
  language as a way to reference visual concepts for zero-shot transfer.
- **2022-04:** Flamingo extends the bridge from image-text alignment to
  assistant-style visual-language prompting, with interleaved images/videos/text
  as input and free-form text as output.
- **2023-03:** The GPT-4 Technical Report describes GPT-4 as a large multimodal
  model accepting image and text inputs and producing text outputs. Section 4.1
  describes prompts with arbitrarily interlaced text and images.
- **2023-09:** OpenAI publishes the GPT-4V(ision) System Card. Vision becomes a
  deployed GPT assistant capability, but the same document foregrounds
  hallucinations, image-borne jailbreaks, medical limitations, and user-facing
  safety boundaries.
- **2023-12:** Google publishes the Gemini technical report, framing Gemini as a
  family trained jointly across text, image, audio, and video and able to accept
  interleaved multimodal inputs.
- **2024-02:** OpenAI publishes "Video generation models as world simulators,"
  introducing Sora as a text-conditional diffusion transformer over video/image
  patches and claiming up-to-one-minute generated videos while explicitly
  listing simulator limitations.
- **2024-05-13:** OpenAI announces GPT-4o. The product page and system card make
  real-time audio/vision/text interaction, low audio latency, and end-to-end
  multimodal training central to the interface story.
- **2024-05-14:** Google announces Veo as a video-generation model capable of
  1080p videos longer than a minute. Use this as official product framing only.
- **2024-08:** The GPT-4o System Card documents the omni model's input/output
  modalities, end-to-end text/vision/audio training, latency claims, and
  voice-specific risk surfaces.
- **2024-12:** The Sora System Card documents deployment preparation, red
  teaming, data-category framing, and moderation/evaluation layers for video
  generation.
