## Research And Product Actors

- **Alec Radford et al. / OpenAI CLIP team:** Use as the research bridge from
  internet-scale language supervision to visual concepts. Do not over-personalize
  beyond the paper's authorship; the key historical role is the method and data
  scale.
- **Jean-Baptiste Alayrac et al. / Flamingo team:** Use as the bridge from
  contrastive vision-language learning to interleaved multimodal prompting and
  few-shot visual-language interaction.
- **OpenAI GPT-4/GPT-4V teams:** Treat as institutional actors behind the
  GPT-style visual assistant turn. The GPT-4 Technical Report and GPT-4V System
  Card are the anchors, not anecdotal launch mythology.
- **Be My Eyes / Be My AI deployment context:** Use only from the GPT-4V System
  Card as a concrete accessibility deployment setting where usefulness and
  hallucination/error limitations appeared together.
- **Google Gemini team:** Use for the source-bound "trained jointly across
  image, audio, video, and text" claim and the interleaved multimodal input
  architecture description.
- **OpenAI GPT-4o team:** Use institutionally for the omni interface claim:
  text/audio/image/video inputs, text/audio/image outputs, and real-time
  speech-to-speech interaction.
- **OpenAI Sora team:** Use for the video frontier: spacetime patches, diffusion
  transformer framing, up-to-one-minute video claims, and the built-in limitation
  caveats.
- **Google DeepMind / Google Research Veo team:** Use as official product
  framing for Google's competing video-generation model. Keep claims Yellow

## Guardrail

Do not write this chapter as a hero story. Most sources are institutional
technical reports and system cards. Individual names should appear only where
they clarify the research lineage; the narrative center is the interface and
architecture shift from text-only chat to mixed media.
