## Notes on dating

- The Williams-tube specifications (0.2-second retention, >5 Hz refresh, 600 μs hypothetical-machine instruction time) are the chapter's most-anchored single set of numbers and are the operative anchor for the "amnesiac machines" scene. The 1947 internal report is the source — not the 1948 *Nature* Letter (which is paywalled past the first paragraph) or the 1949 *Proc. IEE* paper (also paywalled).
- The 1949 dating of Forrester's magnetic-material insight is corroborated across two sources (Forrester's own oral history and the IEEE Pioneers biographical entry); the German-tank-turret provenance is single-source and should be hedged in prose.
- The Wang patent date (1949-10-21 application, 1955-05-17 issue) and the Forrester patent date (1951-05-11 application, 1956-02-28 issue) are face-sheet-anchored and Green. The IBM-purchase-of-Wang-patent figure ($500,000 in 1956) is Yellow pending Pugh 1984 or IBM Archives.
- The "first Whirlwind core memory installation" date is widely cited as August 1953 but is currently Yellow pending a primary anchor in Pugh 1984 or Redmond & Smith 1980.
- The chapter does not assert a single "magnetic core memory was invented on date X" claim. Multiple parallel paths converged in the 1949-1953 window; the chapter's frame is parallel invention, not single-inventor primacy.
