# Brief: Chapter 45 - Generative Adversarial Networks

## Thesis
Ian Goodfellow introduced a mathematical framework that pitted two neural networks against each other in a zero-sum game. This adversarial training mechanism solved the problem of unsupervised generation, allowing machines to hallucinate entirely new, photorealistic images from the latent space.

## Scope
- IN SCOPE: Ian Goodfellow, the 2014 GAN paper, Generator vs. Discriminator, the math of minimax games, the explosion of generative imagery.
- OUT OF SCOPE: Diffusion models (Part 8).

## Scenes Outline
1. **The Bar Argument:** Goodfellow conceives the idea of adversarial networks over drinks with colleagues in Montreal.
2. **The Counterfeiter and the Cop:** Pedagogical explanation of the Generator (counterfeiter) trying to fool the Discriminator (cop).
3. **The Faces that Do Not Exist:** The rapid evolution of GANs to generate high-resolution, photorealistic human faces.
