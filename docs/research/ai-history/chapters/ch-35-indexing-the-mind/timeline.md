## Notes on dating

- **The PageRank paper's date.** The PDF cover lists "January 29, 1998" but the InfoLab tech report numbering convention places it at 1999-66, hence the conventional 1999 citation. Both are honest. The chapter should phrase the date as "the PageRank paper, dated January 29, 1998 on its cover and formally published in 1999 as Stanford InfoLab Tech Report 1999-66."
- **The 1998 Anatomy paper vs. the 1999 PageRank paper.** Confusingly, the WWW7 Anatomy paper (April 1998) cites PageRank as already-existing prior work, but the InfoLab tech report number 1999-66 postdates it. The chapter should not try to settle which "came first" — both papers were drafts being passed around Stanford in 1997-1998 and both reach formal publication on slightly different timelines. The chapter's anchor for the *algorithm* is the PageRank paper (PDF cover Jan 29 1998); the chapter's anchor for the *system* is the Anatomy paper (WWW7 April 1998).
- **Barroso 2003 volume.** The verified citation is *IEEE Micro*, vol. 23, no. 2 (March-April 2003). The legacy chapter stub citation of "vol. 18, no. 2" is incorrect — see `sources.md` Conflict Notes.
- **Founding chronology dates.** September 4, 1998 incorporation, September 15, 1997 domain registration, $100,000 Bechtolsheim check, Stanford 1995 graduate-orientation meeting — all Yellow at contract time. Multiple secondaries converge on these dates but no primary anchors are in the verified set.
- **The "founding date of Google" question.** Multiple plausible dates exist (1996 BackRub crawl start; April 1998 Anatomy paper; September 1998 incorporation; 1999 PageRank paper formal publication). The chapter's stance: 1998 is the load-bearing year (Anatomy paper + PageRank cover date + incorporation). Do not settle which sub-date "founded Google" — different sources legitimately pick different ones.
