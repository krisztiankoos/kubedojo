## Conflict Notes

- **The PageRank paper's date.** The PDF cover lists "January 29, 1998" but the Stanford InfoLab tech report number (1999-66) and the conventional citation form attribute it to 1999. Both can be true: drafted/dated in early 1998, formally numbered as a 1999 InfoLab publication. The chapter should cite both honestly: "The PageRank paper, dated January 29, 1998 on its cover and formally published in 1999 as Stanford InfoLab Tech Report 1999-66."
- **Volume number for Barroso 2003.** The legacy chapter stub cited "IEEE Micro 18, no. 2 (2003)" — *incorrect*. The verified citation is *IEEE Micro*, vol. 23, no. 2 (March-April 2003), pp. 22-28. Verified by the masthead "MARCH-APRIL 2003" on the article's PDF header line and by the journal's standard volume-numbering chronology (IEEE Micro Vol. 1 was 1981).
- **The "1,000 cheap PCs" folklore.** A repeatedly-cited image is "1,000 PCs in a corkboard rack." The Anatomy 1998 paper gives no such number; Barroso 2003 gives 15,000+ commodity PCs as of *2003*, not 1998. The chapter must not mash these timelines together.
