## Sequence Guidance for Prose

1. Open in 1957-1958 Buffalo/Cornell, not with 1969.
2. Move from Rosenblatt's questions in the 1958 paper to the Mark I hardware.
3. Put the press story after the technical program is established, so hype does not define the chapter.
4. Explain convergence and separability through Principles of Neurodynamics before noting the later Ch15 gradient-descent reinterpretation.
5. Close with the live cybernetic alternative and a restrained Ch17 forward-link.

## Chronology Risks

- Do not state a precise Mark I public demonstration date beyond "June 1960" until a direct source resolves June 23 versus the June 24 Navy release.
- Do not call the 1962 Spartan edition the first appearance of Principles; the DTIC/CAL report version is dated March 1961.
- Do not place Rosenblatt at Dartmouth or inside the McCarthy/Minsky symbolic-AI organizing group.
- Do not let the 1969 book supply the chapter's retrospective verdict.
- Do not date Hubel-Wiesel V1 work as "after Rosenblatt" — the 1959 paper is contemporary with `IRE60` and earlier than `POND61`'s consolidated theoretical statement; influence direction remains unsettled.
- Do not date ADALINE as "before" or "after" Mark I — the 1960 Stanford work is contemporaneous with the Mark I demonstration, not a prior or downstream development.
- Do not melodramatize the 1971 sailing-accident date — record it as a one-paragraph historiographic-muting beat, not a tragic coda.
