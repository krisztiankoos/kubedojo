# People: Chapter 14 - The Perceptron

## Principal Figures

### Frank Rosenblatt

- **Role:** Psychologist, head of the perceptron program's intellectual design, author of `PsychRev58` and `POND61`.
- **Institutional setting:** Cornell Aeronautical Laboratory, Buffalo, New York; Cornell University, Ithaca.
- **Verified anchors:** `PsychRev58` p. 386 identifies Rosenblatt at Cornell Aeronautical Laboratory and ONR sponsorship. `POND61` front matter identifies him as director of the Cognitive Systems Research Program; pp. vii-viii clarify that the perceptron program was motivated by brain modeling and natural intelligence, not primarily by building AI devices.
- **Drafting use:** Treat Rosenblatt as serious and technically ambitious. He should not be reduced either to a hype source or to a victim of later critique.
- **Warnings:** Do not call him the inventor of neural networks. Do not attribute the full "walk, talk..." press line to his demonstrated machine capability.

### Charles Wightman

- **Role:** Mark I engineering figure at Cornell Aeronautical Laboratory.
- **Verified anchors:** `POND61` p. ix says the engineering work on Mark I was carried out primarily by Charles Wightman and Francis Martin at CAL.
- **Drafting use:** Use Wightman to make the Mark I machine concrete. The photo-caption claim that he is the project engineer is Yellow until a primary image-caption page is extracted, but his engineering role is Green from Rosenblatt's acknowledgments.
- **Warnings:** Do not make Wightman a decorative name only. Hardware credibility depends on showing that Mark I was built by a team.

### Francis Martin

- **Role:** Mark I engineering figure at CAL.
- **Verified anchors:** `POND61` p. ix names Francis Martin with Wightman for engineering work on Mark I.
- **Drafting use:** Pair with Wightman in the infrastructure paragraph. Mention only where the prose has room; do not create an invented scene.

### John C. Hay

- **Role:** Mark I experimental program figure; co-author of the operator manual.
- **Verified anchors:** `POND61` p. ix says the experimental program with Mark I was carried out by John Hay. `MarkI-Manual60` metadata names John C. Hay and Albert E. Murray as authors of the operator manual.
- **Drafting use:** Use Hay to separate experimental operation from Rosenblatt's theory and Wightman's engineering.

### Albert E. Murray

- **Role:** Co-author of the Mark I operator manual.
- **Verified anchors:** `MarkI-Manual60` metadata.
- **Drafting use:** Worklist figure until operator manual pages are extracted. Do not overbuild a biographical role.

### R. D. Joseph

- **Role:** Perceptron theory contributor and critic within the program.
- **Verified anchors:** `POND61` p. ix says Joseph and H. D. Block contributed ideas, suggestions, and criticisms to an extent that Rosenblatt says could entitle them to co-authorship of several chapters.
- **Drafting use:** Supports the point that Principles of Neurodynamics was not one person's unreviewed speculation.

### H. D. Block

- **Role:** Perceptron theory contributor; later author of a 1962 perceptron review/model paper.
- **Verified anchors:** `POND61` p. ix credits Block alongside Joseph for ideas, suggestions, criticism, and mathematical proofreading.
- **Drafting use:** Mention as part of the mathematical discipline around the program. A richer role needs full Block paper extraction.

### Carl Kesler, Trevor Barker, David Feign, and Louise Hay

- **Verified anchors:** `POND61` p. ix credits them with assistance in programming the various digital computers employed.
- **Drafting use:** Useful in the IBM 704 / Burroughs 220 simulation paragraph. Avoid turning them into unsupported mini-biographies.

### Mark Kac and Barkley Rosser

- **Role:** Cornell faculty administrative/support figures.
- **Verified anchors:** `POND61` p. ix thanks them and other Cornell faculty for administrative support and encouragement.

### W. S. Holmes and Alexander Stieber
