# People: Chapter 5 — The Neural Abstraction

## Verified Protagonists

### Warren Sturgis McCulloch (1898-1969)
- Neurophysiologist and one of two authors of the 1943 paper.
- Education: Haverford College (mathematics), Yale (philosophy and psychology), Columbia (medicine, neurophysiology).
- In 1943 affiliated with the Department of Psychiatry at the Illinois Neuropsychiatric Institute, College of Medicine, University of Illinois — and the University of Chicago. (M-P 1943 p.115 author block: "University of Illinois, College of Medicine, Department of Psychiatry at the Illinois Neuropsychiatric Institute, University of Chicago, Chicago, U.S.A." Verified by `pdftotext` 2026-04-28.)
- 42 years old when he met Pitts, per Gefter 2015 paragraph 7.
- Photograph and biographical sketch in Gefter 2015 (Nautilus); fuller biography pending Conway-Siegelman 2005 page anchors.

### Walter Harry Pitts Jr. (1923-1969)
- Logician and second author of the 1943 paper. Born in Detroit; died in Cambridge, Massachusetts of bleeding esophageal varices on 14 May 1969 (Gefter 2015 final paragraphs).
- No university degree; effectively self-educated via the public-library Russell-Whitehead route reported by Lettvin (Gefter 2015 paragraphs 2-3). Wiener later promised him a PhD at MIT despite his lack of a high-school diploma; Pitts never accepted the degree (Gefter 2015 final paragraphs).
- 18-19 years old when he met McCulloch in the early 1940s (Gefter 2015 paragraph 7).
- Most careful published biography: Smalheiser 2000 in *Perspectives in Biology and Medicine* vol. 43 pp. 217-226 (paywalled).
- The chapter must mark Lettvin-oral-history scenes (1935 library, 1938 Chicago run-away, the Russell letter) as oral-history reconstructions, not documentary facts.

## Verified Supporting Cast

### Jerome Lettvin (1920-2011)
- University of Chicago medical student in the early 1940s; introduced Pitts to McCulloch (Gefter 2015 paragraph 7).
- Source of nearly all the popular-biography scenes about Pitts, recorded as oral history in Anderson & Rosenfeld 2000 *Talking Nets* (MIT Press) and Gefter 2015 ref. 2.
- Co-author of the 1959 "What the Frog's Eye Tells the Frog's Brain" with Pitts, McCulloch, and Maturana — out-of-scope for the chapter except as an honest-close forward-pointer.

### Donald Olding Hebb (1904-1985)
- Canadian psychologist at McGill; author of *The Organization of Behavior: A Neuropsychological Theory* (Wiley, 1949). Source of the "neurophysiological postulate" at p.62.
- The chapter uses Hebb only at the bridge between the 1943 calculus and the future of learning research; do not draft Hebb-biography material.

### Nicolas Rashevsky (1899-1972)
- Mathematical biophysicist at the University of Chicago; founder and editor of the *Bulletin of Mathematical Biophysics* (the journal that published the 1943 paper).
- Anchored at Hebb 1949 p.xv (cited by name as the head of the mathematical-biophysics community of which Pitts and McCulloch were part) and at Piccinini 2004 abstract (the "lively community of biophysicists doing mathematical work on neural networks").
- The chapter mentions Rashevsky as the institutional context, not as a protagonist with scene-level material.

### Norbert Wiener (1894-1964)
- MIT mathematician; later founder of cybernetics. Recruits Pitts to MIT in 1943.
- The chapter touches Wiener only at the boundary of Scenes 1 and the honest close (the December 1943 letter; the 1945 von Neumann *First Draft* citing only M-P 1943 was made possible by the Wiener-cybernetics circulation of the paper). Cybernetics-as-movement is reserved for Ch6.

### Bertrand Russell (1872-1970)
- Co-author with Whitehead of *Principia Mathematica*; intellectual figure for both McCulloch and Pitts.
- The chapter mentions Russell as the figure (a) whose *Principia* notation M-P 1943 borrows on p.118, and (b) at the centre of the Lettvin-oral-history Pitts scenes (1935 library, 1938 Chicago run-away). Use bibliographic anchor only; do not draft from Russell-biography material.

### Rudolf Carnap (1891-1970)
- Logician and philosopher; author of *The Logical Syntax of Language* (1938), the symbolic apparatus M-P 1943 explicitly adopts on p.118.
- Anchored at M-P 1943 p.118 and p.131. The 1947 letter from McCulloch to Carnap (cataloguing Pitts's range, quoted in Gefter 2015) is admissible as Yellow.

### John von Neumann (1903-1957)
- Author of *First Draft of a Report on the EDVAC* (June 1945); per Gefter 2015 the report cites only "A Logical Calculus" by McCulloch and Pitts among neural-modelling sources.
- The chapter touches von Neumann only at the honest close, as evidence of the 1943 paper's downstream reach into computer architecture.

## Bibliographic-only Mentions

The chapter may name but must not draft scene-level material from:

- Alfred North Whitehead (1861-1947) — *Principia Mathematica* co-author.
- Stephen Kleene (1909-1994) — read the 1943 paper as the seed of finite-automata theory in his 1956 "Representation of Events in Nerve Nets and Finite Automata"; out-of-scope but worth a bibliographic forward-pointer if Aizawa & Schlatter 2008 is later anchored.
- Frank Rosenblatt (1928-1971) — perceptron designer; out-of-scope (Ch14).
- Margaret Wiener — the wife of Norbert Wiener whose 1952 invention about McCulloch and her daughter caused Wiener to break with Pitts (Gefter 2015; Conway-Siegelman 2005). Out-of-scope for the chapter.
- Humberto Maturana (1928-2021) — co-author of the 1959 frog's-eye paper. Out-of-scope.
- Householder, Landahl — listed at Hebb 1949 p.xv as part of the Rashevsky community. Mentioned only to underscore that the 1943 paper was not isolated work.
