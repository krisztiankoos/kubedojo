# Timeline: Chapter 5 — The Neural Abstraction

| Date | Event | Verification |
|---|---|---|
| 1898 (b.) | Warren Sturgis McCulloch born in Orange, New Jersey. | Yellow. Standard biographical claim; the chapter relies on Gefter 2015 (Nautilus paragraphs 5 and 7, which give "42 years old when he met Pitts" and the 1923 mention of his Columbia studies). |
| 1910-1913 | Russell and Whitehead publish the first edition of *Principia Mathematica* (3 vols., Cambridge University Press). | Green as a bibliographic fact; the M-P 1943 paper cites the second edition (1925). |
| 1923 (b.) | Walter Harry Pitts Jr. born in Detroit, Michigan. | Yellow. Date repeated by Gefter 2015 paragraph 5 ("In 1923, the year that Walter Pitts was born…"); Smalheiser 2000 metadata corroborates via vol.43 (2000) of *Perspectives in Biology and Medicine* but article body paywalled. |
| 1923 | McCulloch is at Columbia, studying "experimental aesthetics" and finishing a medical degree in neurophysiology. | Yellow. Source: Gefter 2015 paragraph 5. |
| 1925 | Russell and Whitehead's *Principia Mathematica* second edition appears (cited at M-P 1943 p.131 literature list). | Green as a bibliographic fact. |
| 1935 | According to Lettvin's later oral history, the 12-year-old Pitts hides in a Detroit public library to escape neighbourhood bullies, encounters *Principia Mathematica*, reads it cover to cover over three days, identifies errors, and writes to Russell, who replies inviting him to study at Cambridge. | Yellow. Lettvin-oral-history reconstruction reported in Gefter 2015 paragraph 3 (Nautilus), with Gefter citing *Talking Nets* (Anderson & Rosenfeld 2000). The chapter must label this scene as Lettvin-oral-history-derived, not documentary. |
| 1938 | Carnap publishes *The Logical Syntax of Language* (New York: Harcourt-Brace). | Green via M-P 1943 p.131 bibliographic citation. |
| 1938 | According to Lettvin's oral history, on hearing Russell would visit the University of Chicago, the 15-year-old Pitts runs from Detroit to Chicago, never to see his family again. | Yellow. Same Lettvin chain as 1935; same hedging applies. |
| Early 1940s | Pitts has been "hanging around the University of Chicago, working a menial job and sneaking into Russell's lectures." Jerome Lettvin (then a Chicago medical student) introduces him to Warren McCulloch. | Yellow. Source: Gefter 2015 paragraph 7. |
| 1942-1943 | Pitts moves into McCulloch's household in Hinsdale, Illinois, and the two work together on what becomes "A Logical Calculus of the Ideas Immanent in Nervous Activity." | Yellow. Source: Gefter 2015 (Hinsdale paragraphs). The chapter may note the move without inventing scene-level dialogue. |
| 1943 | McCulloch and Pitts publish "A Logical Calculus of the Ideas Immanent in Nervous Activity" in the *Bulletin of Mathematical Biophysics*, vol. 5, pp. 115-133. The paper sets out the threshold-logic neuron, the five physical assumptions, the Carnap-syntax notation $N_i(t)$, and Theorem 7 ("Alterable synapses can be replaced by circles"). | **Green**. Verified by `pdftotext -layout` on the 1990 reprint, 2026-04-28; the bibliographic and content claims trace to specific page anchors (p.115 abstract, p.117 learning paragraph, p.118 Section 2 opening, p.124 Theorem 7, pp.130-131 Section 4 and literature list). |
| Fall 1943 | Wiener offers Pitts a PhD path at MIT under his supervision; Pitts moves to Cambridge, MA as a "special student." | Yellow. Source: Gefter 2015. The MIT Wiener period is downstream of the chapter's main scope; one-sentence forward-pointer only. |
| December 1943 | Pitts writes to McCulloch from MIT: "I now understand at once some seven-eighths of what Wiener says, which I am told is something of an achievement." | Yellow. Source: Gefter 2015 ref. 1, citing McCulloch Papers BM139 (American Philosophical Society). Letter quoted in Gefter; APS cross-anchoring not yet attempted. |
| June 1945 | Von Neumann issues the *First Draft of a Report on the EDVAC* (Moore School of Electrical Engineering, University of Pennsylvania, 30 June 1945). The report cites M-P 1943 explicitly at Section 4.2 (p.12 of the document): "Following W. Pitts and W. S. McCulloch ('A logical calculus of the ideas immanent in nervous activity', Bull. Math. Bio-physics, Vol. 5 (1943), pp 115-133) we ignore the more complicated aspects of neuron functioning…" — and a full-document scan confirms it is the only journal citation in the report. | **Green**. Verified by Claude `curl` + `pdftotext -layout` on the Internet Archive scan (`https://archive.org/download/firstdraftofrepo00vonn/firstdraftofrepo00vonn.pdf`) 2026-04-28. |
| 1947 | McCulloch and Pitts publish "How We Know Universals: The Perception of Auditory and Visual Forms" in *Bulletin of Mathematical Biophysics* vol. 9, pp. 127-147. The chapter does not draft from this paper; one-sentence forward-pointer only. | Yellow as a bibliographic forward-pointer. |
| 1949 | Hebb publishes *The Organization of Behavior: A Neuropsychological Theory* (New York: John Wiley & Sons). The Introduction (p.xv) places his work alongside the Rashevsky-Pitts-Householder-Landahl-McCulloch mathematical-biophysics line. Chapter 4 (pp.60-62, "The First Stage of Perception: Growth of the Assembly") contains the "neurophysiological postulate" at p.62. | **Green**. Verified by page-by-page `pdftotext` 2026-04-28 on the Max Planck PDF. |
| 1957-1958 | Frank Rosenblatt begins work on the perceptron at the Cornell Aeronautical Laboratory. The chapter does not draft this; the algorithmic-learning story is reserved for Ch14. | Out-of-scope forward-pointer only. |
| 1959 | Lettvin, Maturana, McCulloch, and Pitts publish "What the Frog's Eye Tells the Frog's Brain" in the *Proceedings of the IRE*. According to Gefter 2015 (citing Lettvin oral history in *Talking Nets*), the experimental finding that the eye does substantial pre-brain interpretive work disturbed Pitts's earlier purely-logical reading of the 1943 paper. | Yellow. One-sentence honest-close forward-pointer; the chapter does not draft from the 1959 paper itself. |
| 1969-05-14 | Walter Pitts dies in Cambridge, Massachusetts, of bleeding esophageal varices. | Yellow. Source: Gefter 2015 (final paragraphs). |
| 1969 | Warren McCulloch dies four months later. | Yellow. Source: Gefter 2015 (final paragraphs). |
| 1990 | The 1943 paper is reprinted in the *Bulletin of Mathematical Biology* vol. 52, pp. 99-115. The reprint is the digital copy widely circulated today. | Green. Reprint header explicitly states "Reprinted from the *Bulletin of Mathematical Biophysics*, Vol. 5, pp. 115-133 (1943)." Verified 2026-04-28. |
| 2000 | Anderson and Rosenfeld publish *Talking Nets: An Oral History of Neural Networks* (MIT Press), the volume Gefter cites for Lettvin's oral history of Pitts. Smalheiser publishes "Walter Pitts" in *Perspectives in Biology and Medicine* vol. 43, pp. 217-226 — the most careful published Pitts biography, paywalled at JHU MUSE. | Yellow as bibliographic anchors. |
| 2004 | Piccinini publishes "The First Computational Theory of Mind and Brain: A Close Look at McCulloch and Pitts's 'Logical Calculus of Ideas Immanent in Nervous Activity'" in *Synthese* vol. 141, pp. 175-215. Abstract anchored at Springer; body paywalled. | **Green** at abstract level; Yellow at paragraph level. |
| 2015 | Gefter publishes "The Man Who Tried to Redeem the World with Logic" in *Nautilus*, issue 21. The chapter's principal popular-narrative source for Pitts's biography. | **Green** as a secondary narrative source. Verified by Claude `curl` 2026-04-28. |
