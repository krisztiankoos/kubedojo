## Conflict Notes

- **Priority of "stochastic gradient descent."** The 1951 Robbins-Monro paper does not say "stochastic gradient descent"; that label is later (commonly attributed to the 1960s–1970s mathematical-statistics literature, with Bottou's 1990s expositions popularizing it for ML). The chapter's framing must be careful: Robbins-Monro gave the *root-finding-with-noisy-observations* framework; Widrow-Hoff 1960 gave a *gradient-of-MSE* learning rule on physical hardware; only modern hindsight calls both "instances of SGD." Do not anachronize.
- **Did Widrow-Hoff *know* they were doing what Cauchy had done?** Yes, explicitly — they cite "the method of steepest descent" by name (`WidrowHoff60` p. 98). The chapter's prose can use this fact, but should not over-dramatize it as an act of historical reverence; "method of steepest descent" was standard numerical-analysis vocabulary in 1960 and Widrow uses it as such, not as homage.
- **Did Linnainmaa's 1970 thesis "invent backpropagation"?** No, and the chapter must not say so. The honest claim, anchored to `Schmidhuber15` p. 91, is: Linnainmaa gave the first explicit, efficient reverse-mode chain-rule machinery on arbitrary computational graphs. Backpropagation as a *learning algorithm for neural networks* came later (Werbos 1981 NN-specific application, Rumelhart-Hinton-Williams 1986 popularization — both Ch24's territory).
- **Schmidhuber's history is itself contested.** Schmidhuber 2015 has been criticized for over-attributing priority to non-English-language sources (Ivakhnenko, Linnainmaa). The chapter should follow his Linnainmaa attribution because (a) it is the cleanest English-language anchor, (b) the underlying claim about reverse-mode AD is mathematically uncontroversial, and (c) the 1976 BIT paper publicly archives the same machinery. But the chapter should *not* take Schmidhuber as the only source on contested priority disputes (e.g., his Ivakhnenko advocacy in Section 5.3 is not Ch15's territory and is not adopted here).
- **Hadamard 1908 as gradient-descent precursor.** Schmidhuber's parenthetical is the only anchor; the Hadamard paper is on the equilibrium of clamped elastic plates and the connection to "gradient descent on errors" is at best a stretch. Do not draft a Hadamard scene; treat as Schmidhuber's idiosyncratic attribution.
