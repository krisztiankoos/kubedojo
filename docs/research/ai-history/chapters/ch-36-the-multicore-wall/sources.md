## Conflict Notes

- **"4 GHz" vs Tejas's actual sample clock.** Legacy prose conflates two distinct facts (Tejas samples at 2.8 GHz; the Pentium 4 *line target* of 4 GHz). The chapter must keep them separate. See `open-questions.md` Q1.
- **Thermal vs single-core-economic framing of the cancellation.** Intel's on-record May 7 2004 framing was economic; the engineering-history consensus that hardened by 2006 was thermal/leakage. Both are correct in their own register. See `open-questions.md` Q2.
- **The "nuclear reactor" comparison.** Often misattributed to Borkar 1999 in popular accounts; primary-text verification has not been performed. The chapter must not reproduce this comparison without an anchored primary source.
- **Olukotun-priority vs Intel-economics framings of the multicore pivot.** Both readings are defensible; the chapter must present both, not collapse them.
