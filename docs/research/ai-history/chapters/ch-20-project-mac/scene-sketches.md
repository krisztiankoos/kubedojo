# Scene Sketches: Chapter 20 - Project MAC

## Scene 1: The Computer Utility

Open with the contrast between batch computing and the utility dream. A user at
a terminal is no longer submitting cards and waiting; they are conversing with a
machine that remembers files, links tools, and responds while they think.

## Scene 2: A Project, Not a Lab

Show Project MAC as an organizational hack. It is deliberately interdepartmental
because the research question is not only "can we build a system?" but "what
happens when many fields use online computing every day?"

## Scene 3: CTSS Becomes a Community

Use Fano's concrete CTSS details: terminals across campus and homes, commands,
passwords, links to other users' files, online manuals, and user-developed
programs becoming public commands. This is where "software community" becomes
visible.

## Scene 4: Multics and ITS

Split the road. Multics pursues the reliable computer utility; the AI group
builds an ITS world where PDP-6/PDP-10 programs, eyes, hands, and real-time
control matter more than ordinary service computing.

## Scene 5: Knowledge in the Machine

Move from infrastructure to AI content: MACLISP, MATHLAB/MACSYMA, PLANNER, and
robotics/vision are possible because the machine is interactive enough to let
researchers iteratively build knowledge-rich programs.

## Scene 6: The Network Enters

Introduce ARPANET work as an extension of the utility idea. Resource sharing
now means protocols, remote login, file transfer, network mail, and experiments
between ITS and Multics.

## Scene 7: The Split and the Handoff

Close with reorganization and split: the AI Lab and LCS emerge from the same
machine room. Expert-system commercialization and Lisp-machine hardware are now
set up, but they belong to Ch21 and Ch22.
