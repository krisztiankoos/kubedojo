# Infrastructure Log: Chapter 20 - Project MAC

## Machines and Operating Systems

- CTSS: IBM 7094 time-sharing foundation. In Ch20, CTSS is the practical proof
  that interactive computing could support a community of users, files,
  commands, editing, compiling, debugging, accounting, and shared tools.
- Multics: second-generation utility-scale system, initially a GE/Bell/MIT
  development effort. Use it as the "reliable computer utility" branch of the
  Project MAC story.
- ITS: AI group's PDP-6/PDP-10 environment. Use it as the "high-interaction AI"
  branch, with special calls for real-time control, mechanical hands, computer
  eyes, and remote-control devices.
- PDP-6/PDP-10: the AI workhorse architecture. Mention as the environment for
  ITS, MACLISP, MATHLAB/MACSYMA, and later Lisp-machine pressure, but leave
  Lisp-machine commercialization to Ch22.

## Languages and Systems

- MACLISP: AI group's high-level language; relevant because it tied editing,
  debugging, display packages, compiler work, and arithmetic needs to active AI
  projects.
- MATHLAB/MACSYMA: symbolic algebra environment. This is the strongest example
  of an interactive knowledge-rich system before commercial expert systems.
- PLANNER: automatic-programming / knowledge-based programming current. Use it
  as a bridge from infrastructure to symbolic AI methods; do not turn Ch20 into
  a PLANNER technical chapter.
- MUDDLE/CALICO: programming-technology environments in Progress Report X. Use
  only if prose needs to show that Project MAC was broadening into software
  laboratories and networked programming environments.

## Network and Utility Infrastructure

- Fano's utility idea includes shared files, links, commands, terminals,
  passwords, accounting, online manuals, and community-developed tools.
- ARPANET work in Progress Reports VIII/X shows the utility idea moving beyond
  one campus: NCP, Telnet/logger/ICP, FTP, Multics network login, network mail,
  and ITS-to-Multics experiments.
- Keep "network" as resource sharing and remote use in this chapter. Do not
  jump ahead to open-source culture, web-scale corpora, or modern cloud.

## Prose Guardrails

- Write infrastructure as lived research practice: edit, run, debug, share,
  link, display, interact, and recover state. Avoid a bare acronym parade.
- Contrast Multics and ITS without caricature. Multics aimed at dependable
  utility service; ITS served a smaller AI community's real-time/high-control
  needs.
- Keep the narrative pointed toward expert-system commercialization: Ch20 should
  explain why symbolic AI had a machine room capable of sustaining large,
  interactive, knowledge-heavy programs.
