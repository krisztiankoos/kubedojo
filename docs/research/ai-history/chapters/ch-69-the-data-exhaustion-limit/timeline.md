# Timeline: Chapter 69 - The Data Exhaustion Limit

- 2022-03: Hoffmann et al. publish Chinchilla, arguing compute-optimal language
  training should scale model size and training tokens together.
- 2022-11: Villalobos et al. first post the data-exhaustion analysis.
- 2022-12: Self-Instruct appears, showing a model-generated instruction-data
  pipeline for alignment research.
- 2023-05: Muennighoff et al. post data-constrained scaling work on repeated
  data and finite corpora.
- 2023-06: Phi-1 paper shows high-quality web data plus synthetic textbook and
  exercise data can produce strong code performance at small scale.
- 2023-03/2024-03: GPT-4 technical report and later revisions discuss benchmark
  contamination checks, BIG-bench exclusion, GSM-8K handling, and synthetic
  comparison data for hallucination mitigation.
- 2024-04: Meta releases Llama 3 model card with 15T+ public-source pretraining
  tokens and over 10M human-annotated fine-tuning examples.
- 2024-06: Villalobos et al. v2 / Epoch update states public human text could be
  fully utilized between 2026 and 2032 if trends continue.
- 2024-06/2024-10: FineWeb introduces a 15T-token Common Crawl-derived dataset
  and 1.3T-token FineWeb-Edu subset.
- 2024-07/2024 Nature: Shumailov et al. popularize "model collapse" as a
  recursive synthetic-data risk.
- 2025-04: LiveBench ICLR version frames benchmark contamination as a reason for
  monthly updated, recent-source, objectively scored evaluations.
