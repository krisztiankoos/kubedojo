# Infrastructure Log: Chapter 70 - The Energy Grid Collision

- **Power is location-specific:** Unlike abstract FLOPs, electricity must be
  delivered through local substations, transmission, generation, backup systems,
  and cooling infrastructure.
- **Capacity versus energy:** EPRI's training-cluster forecasts are power
  capacity/peak draw, not annual energy consumption. Do not mix GW with TWh.
- **Physical versus contractual supply:** IEA distinguishes physical electricity
  consumed from clean-energy contracts. Use this distinction whenever PPAs appear.
- **Firm 24/7 supply:** Nuclear procurement appears because data centers value
  carbon-free power that is not weather-dependent. Keep permitting/timing
  caveats.
- **Flexible load:** Inference can sometimes route in time or geography; training
  is less flexible. The mitigation is real but partial.
- **Regional bottlenecks:** Global percentages can hide local strain. The same
  load can be manageable in one grid and a bottleneck in another.
