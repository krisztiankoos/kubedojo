  system-level demand/supply framing rather than individual personalities.

  present as technical forecasters translating scaling into grid quantities.

- PJM, PPL, Susquehanna Nuclear, Talen, AWS, Exelon, and AEP: institutional

  company statements; do not invent private motives.
- Utility planners, interconnection engineers, local regulators: composite
  institutional actors. Do not invent dialogue, but the narrative can show their
  constraints through the sourced timelines and approval caveats.

## Narrative Handling

This is an infrastructure chapter. The protagonists are clocks, wires, and
procurement offices as much as people. Human tension should come from planning
mismatches: a model team wants capacity this decade; a utility sees queues,
permits, turbines, transformers, and ratepayers.
