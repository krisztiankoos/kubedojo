# People: Chapter 12 - Logic Theorist and GPS

## Main Figures

### Allen Newell (1927-1992)

- **Role:** Co-designer of LT and GPS; RAND researcher who brought non-numerical computing, organizational simulation, and symbolic-processing ambitions into the collaboration.
- **Dates for chapter:** mid-November 1954 Selfridge-triggered turn; 1955-1959 LT/GPS work; 1991 oral-history retrospective.
- **Primary source:** `P-868`, p. 1; `P-1584`, p. 1; `CBI91`, pp. 3-12.
- **Drafting note:** Use Newell's oral history as retrospective evidence, not as a transcript of his 1950s inner thoughts.

### Herbert A. Simon (1916-2001)

- **Role:** Co-designer of LT and GPS; Carnegie Tech organizational theorist and cognitive scientist; later key autobiographical source for LT/GPS scenes.
- **Dates for chapter:** 1955-1961 core LT/GPS period; 1991 autobiography is a source target.
- **Primary source:** `P-868`, p. 1; `P-1584`, p. 1; `GPS61`, p. 109.
- **Drafting note:** `Simon-Models` is needed before using typewriter/Theorem 2.01 and JSL rejection anecdotes.

### J. C. "Cliff" Shaw

- **Role:** RAND programmer and implementation partner; central to making LT/GPS programmable rather than only a conceptual specification.
- **Dates for chapter:** 1950s RAND work with Newell; 1957 LT programming paper; 1959 GPS paper.
- **Primary source:** `P-868`, p. 1 credits Shaw for undertaking LT computer realization; `P-1584`, p. 1 names Shaw as co-author; `CBI91`, pp. 5-6 describes Newell going to Shaw while building non-numerical simulations.
- **Drafting note:** Do not reduce Shaw to "coder." The infrastructure story depends on his programming-language and realization work.

### Oliver Selfridge

- **Role:** Catalyst figure for Newell's turn toward pattern recognition, adaptive processing, and symbolic information processing.
- **Dates for chapter:** mid-November 1954 RAND talk per Newell's oral history.
- **Primary source:** `CBI91`, p. 9.
- **Drafting note:** A short catalyst scene is supportable, but avoid mystical conversion prose beyond Newell's own retrospective framing.

### John McCarthy

- **Role:** Dartmouth naming figure and Ch11 bridge; not a main Ch12 technical actor.
- **Dates for chapter:** 1956 Dartmouth context.
- **Primary source:** Ch11 `sources.md`; `CBI91`, pp. 10-12 for Newell's recollection of McCarthy and the naming moment.
- **Drafting note:** Keep McCarthy mostly in cross-link prose. Ch13 owns LISP.

### Marvin Minsky

- **Role:** Dartmouth participant and symbolic/neural context figure; useful for contrasting MIT's trajectory with Newell-Simon-Shaw.
- **Dates for chapter:** 1956 Dartmouth and early AI field formation.
- **Primary source:** `CBI91`, pp. 10-13.
- **Drafting note:** Do not let the Dartmouth ensemble pull the chapter away from LT/GPS.

### Claude Shannon

- **Role:** Information-theory symposium and Dartmouth network figure; contextual bridge between Ch11 and Ch12.
- **Dates for chapter:** 1956 Information Theory Symposium and Dartmouth contacts.
- **Primary source:** `P-868`, p. 1 footnote; `CBI91`, p. 11.
- **Drafting note:** Use only as connective tissue unless correspondence anchors are later found.

### Alfred North Whitehead and Bertrand Russell

- **Role:** Authors of *Principia Mathematica*, the source of the sentential-calculus theorem domain LT attacked.
- **Dates for chapter:** 1925 second edition cited by `P-868`; 1950s as task corpus.
- **Primary source:** `P-868`, p. 8 cites *Principia Mathematica* and explains that LT uses Whitehead and Russell's proposition numbering.
- **Drafting note:** They are task-domain figures, not active scene participants.
