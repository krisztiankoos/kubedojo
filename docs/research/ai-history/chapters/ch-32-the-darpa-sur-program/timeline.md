The report effectively ends US government funding for machine-translation research for over a decade. | **Green** — verified via Church 2018 NLE tribute to Charles Wayne and Jelinek 2005 LREC slides PDF p.3 (which lists Pierce's role as "Head of the 1966 Automatic Language Processing Committee of Defense Dept."). |

SDC pursues its own system. | **Green** for the five-year duration and the multi-contractor structure — verified via Newell 1971 Section 1.1 Conclusion 2; CMU 1977 report Section I (PDF p.7); Lowerre & Reddy 1979 Section 15-2; Juang & Rabiner 2005 PDF p.8-9. |

| 13 August 1976 | The HARPY system at CMU becomes operational at the 1,011-word document-retrieval task — the configuration that would be demonstrated to ARPA. | **Green** — verified via Lowerre & Reddy 1979 Figure 15-1: "Operational 13 August 1976." |
| September 1976 | The four contractor SUR systems (HARPY, HEARSAY-II, BBN HWIM, SDC) are demonstrated to ARPA at the conclusion of the five-year program. | First version September 1976; present version August 1977 | Carnegie-Mellon University publishes *Speech Understanding Systems: Summary of Results of the Five-Year Research Effort at Carnegie-Mellon University*. | December 1977 | Dennis Klatt publishes "Review of the ARPA Speech Understanding Project" in *The Journal of the Acoustical Society of America* 62(6): 1345-1366. DOI: `10.1121/1.381666`. | 1975 — 1986 | The "funding winter" in US ASR/MT research. DARPA does not fund speech-understanding research at the SUR scale during this period. | **Green** for the framing — Church 2018 NLE tribute to Charles Wayne explicitly characterizes "a funding winter from 1975 to 1986, where there was no US research funding for machine translation or speech recognition." |
| 1979/1980 | B. Lowerre and R. Reddy publish "The HARPY Speech Understanding System" as Chapter 15 of W. A. Lea (ed.), *Trends in Speech Recognition* (Prentice-Hall, 1980). | **Green** — verified via the chapter PDF in the Newell Papers (CMU iiif library mirror). |

| 1985 | At an Arden House workshop, Robert Mercer (or Mercer at a session Jelinek references) utters "There is no data like more data," subsequently treated as the IBM group's methodological maxim. | **Green** for the date and venue — verified via Jelinek 2005 LREC slides PDF p.5. The exact wording is Jelinek's transmitted attribution; no other 1985 primary anchor. |
| Mid-1980s | Charles Wayne, at DARPA, restarts US speech-and-language funding via what becomes known as the "common task method": well-defined objective evaluation, neutral evaluator (NIST), and shared datasets. | **Green** — verified via Church 2018 NLE tribute to Charles Wayne. |
| December 1988 | At the Workshop on Evaluation of NLP Systems, Wayne PA, Frederick Jelinek delivers a talk titled "Applying Information Theoretic Methods: Evaluation of Grammar Quality" in which he first utters the phrase "Whenever I fire a linguist our system performance improves." | **Green** — verified via Jelinek 2005 LREC slides PDF p.2 ("The Quote" slide), where Jelinek himself documents the December 1988 / Wayne PA provenance. |

## Notes on dating

- The chapter's hinge dates are 1971 (Newell charter), September 1976 (four-system demonstration), and December 1988 (the Jelinek quote). Each is independently anchored. Prose should be careful to keep the December 1988 quote distinct from the SUR period; retrojecting it as in-period commentary is a chronological error.
- The "funding winter" date range (1975-1986) is Church 2018's framing. It overlaps with — but does not equal — the SUR program's 1971-1976 timeline; SUR was the *terminus* of the late-1960s/early-1970s ASR funding wave, not the start of the funding winter.
- The 1973 North-Holland publication of the Newell report is the same content as the May 1971 CMU technical report, per Bahl/Jelinek/Mercer 1983 Reference [21]. Cite the May 1971 technical report when anchoring page numbers; cite the 1973 North-Holland book when discussing institutional reception.
