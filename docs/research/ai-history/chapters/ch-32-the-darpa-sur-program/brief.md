# Brief: Chapter 32 - The DARPA SUR Program

## Thesis
The transition of AI from a theoretical, rule-based academic pursuit to an empirical, data-driven engineering discipline was forced by military funding metrics. The DARPA Speech Understanding Research (SUR) program mandated strict, statistical evaluation, ending the era of elegant, hand-coded logic in favor of what actually worked on data.

## Scope
- IN SCOPE: DARPA's funding shifts, the creation of standardized evaluation metrics, the tension between linguists and statisticians (Fred Jelinek's famous quote).
- OUT OF SCOPE: The deep mathematical derivations of Hidden Markov Models (covered in Part 5).

## Scenes Outline
1. **The Linguist's Failure:** Early attempts to hand-code speech recognition rules break under the chaos of real human accents and noise.
2. **The DARPA Mandate:** DARPA demands measurable, empirical results rather than theoretical papers, creating the first standardized test sets.
3. **"Every time I fire a linguist..."** Fred Jelinek and the IBM team embrace statistical models, proving that dumb data and probabilities beat elegant linguistic theory.
