## Conflict Notes

- **The "fire a linguist" quote** is widely circulated in mutated forms. Jelinek's own 2005 LREC retrospective gives the verifiable December 1988 / Wayne PA provenance and the wording "Whenever I fire a linguist our system performance improves." Use Jelinek's wording. Do not introduce the more famous variant ("Every time I fire a linguist…") in prose without an editorial note flagging it as a paraphrase.
- **The "four SUR contractor systems"** — sources agree on HARPY, HEARSAY-II, BBN HWIM, SDC. Some sources also count CMU Dragon as a SUR-era system but it predates the September 1976 demonstration. Keep the chapter's "four systems demonstrated" framing; flag Dragon separately as the within-SUR statistical-methods exception per Jelinek 2005.
- **HARPY exceeded vs. met spec** — Lowerre & Reddy 1979 Figure 15-1 reads "Harpy not only met all the specifications but exceeded several of the stated objectives" (Section 15-2). The CMU 1977 report uses the more measured "It not only satisfies the original goals, but exceeds some of the stated objectives" (PDF p.7). Both phrasings are anchored. Use the CMU 1977 wording.
- **Was SUR a success or a failure?** — Newell 1971's own framing was that the program would be successful if at least one contractor met the Final Specifications. By that criterion, SUR succeeded (HARPY met them). The "DARPA disappointment / funding cut" framing is a separate institutional-history claim about post-1976 program decisions, not a verdict on the 1976 demonstration. Keep these two threads distinct in prose.
- **Was the IBM team part of the SUR program?** — No. IBM was running its Continuous Speech Recognition Group at Yorktown Heights independently of the SUR contracts.
