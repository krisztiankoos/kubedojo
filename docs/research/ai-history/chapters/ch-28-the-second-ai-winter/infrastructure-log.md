# Infrastructure Log: Chapter 28 - The Second AI Winter

## Compute and Hardware

- Specialized AI machines and Lisp environments belong in the chapter only if sourced. The current contract has better anchors for DARPA's shift toward general high-performance architectures than for the commercial Lisp-machine collapse.
- Roland/Shiman p.285 gives the infrastructure pivot: Strategic Computing's machine-intelligence ambition was absorbed into HPC and networking.

## Knowledge Infrastructure

- Expert systems needed not only inference engines but also workflows for extracting, representing, validating, and updating knowledge.
- Rajaram 1987 p.26-14 and Atkinson 1990 p.161 anchor the human bottleneck: domain experts knew the "what," AI technologists knew the "how," and tacit heuristics were hard to verbalize.

## Maintenance Infrastructure

- XCON is the central maintenance case. Neumann 1988 p.34 records 6,200+ rules over ~20,000 parts with about half of rules expected to change each year.
- Neumann 1988 pp.36-37 shows the software-engineering lesson: rule-based programming lacked clean control structure at XCON's scale, causing intelligibility and rule-order problems.

## Institutional Infrastructure

- DARPA Strategic Computing linked AI, robotics, architectures, applications, and military transition. By the late 1980s, leadership pressure shifted from vision to measurable products, transition paths, and maturity.
- Roland/Shiman pp.274-281 anchor the tension between AI ambition, budget pressure, congressional expectations, and producer-consumer technology transfer.

## Prose Guardrail

The winter should read as a failure of surrounding infrastructure to scale with narrow AI successes. That is more accurate than saying "symbolic AI failed" and more useful for the book's infrastructure thesis.
