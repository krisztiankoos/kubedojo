# Brief: Chapter 39 - The Vision Wall

## Thesis
By the late 2000s, machine learning algorithms were theoretically sound, but computer vision was stalling. The limiting infrastructure was not the algorithms, but the datasets (like PASCAL VOC), which were too small and lacked the real-world variance required to train robust models without severe overfitting.

## Scope
- IN SCOPE: The state of computer vision pre-2010, the PASCAL VOC challenge, the problem of overfitting, the plateau in error rates.
- OUT OF SCOPE: ImageNet's creation (next chapter) and AlexNet (Part 7).

## Scenes Outline
1. **The Stagnation:** Computer vision researchers painstakingly tuning features (SIFT, HOG) by hand.
2. **The Benchmark:** PASCAL VOC provides a standardized test, but the algorithms plateau at poor accuracy levels.
3. **The Overfitting Trap:** Models perform well on the tiny training sets but fail completely on novel, real-world images. The realization that small data is the hard wall.
