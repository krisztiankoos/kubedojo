# Timeline: Chapter 41 - The Graphics Hack

- **2001** - SIGGRAPH publishes Lindholm, Kilgard, and Moreton's "A User-Programmable Vertex Engine," part of the programmable-shader turn in consumer graphics hardware. Status: Yellow for this contract until exact page extraction is done.
- **2003** - Mark, Glanville, Akeley, and Kilgard publish "Cg: A System for Programming Graphics Hardware in a C-like Language," documenting the shader-language layer that made programmable graphics more accessible. Status: Yellow; useful context, not load-bearing.
- **2004** - Oh and Jung publish "GPU implementation of neural networks" in *Pattern Recognition*, mapping MLP operations to matrix operations and implementing them on an ATI Radeon 9700 Pro. Anchors: sources.md C09-C10, C17.
- **2004** - Buck et al. publish "Brook for GPUs," presenting GPU computation as stream kernels and compiling higher-level programs to graphics hardware. Anchors: C11, C15, C19-C20.
- **2005** - Steinkraus, Buck, and Simard publish "Using GPUs for Machine Learning Algorithms" at ICDAR, applying DirectX 9 vertex/pixel shaders to machine-learning primitives and a handwriting-recognition workload. Anchors: C01-C05, C07-C08, C12-C14, C18.
- **2007** - Owens et al. publish the *Computer Graphics Forum* GPGPU survey, retrospectively synthesizing how general computation was mapped to programmable graphics hardware. Anchors: C06, C16.
- **2009** - Raina, Madhavan, and Ng publish large-scale deep unsupervised learning with GPUs. Status: Yellow here and mostly a Ch42/later bridge, not a Ch41 center.
