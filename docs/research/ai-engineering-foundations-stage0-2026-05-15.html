3. Where the two research agents converged

  1. Multi-module sub-track is correct, not a single fundamentals module per topic. The current 1.6 (786 lines covering 7 techniques) and 2.1 (705 lines covering 7 principles) are at the rubric floor but cannot reach Bloom L3+ depth across all sub-topics. Splitting each lets focused 600–800-line modules teach individual sub-topics deeply.
  2. Linear sequencing: prompt → context → harness → Symphony. Gemini cited Bloom L1-L4 progression. Codex framed it as "expansion of developer control from single chat to autonomous agentic system." Same answer arrived from different reasoning.
  3. Context engineering is genuinely distinct from prompt eng and from RAG. Gemini: "It is the RAM management of the LLM OS… distinct from RAG because it focuses on session-level state and repo-level knowledge structure, not just retrieving external documents." Codex implicitly agreed by treating it as a peer layer in the triplet.
